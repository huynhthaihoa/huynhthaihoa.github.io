### Refined PhD Research Description

The proposed PhD research aims to develop a unified Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM) and state estimation framework for resource-constrained bio-inspired robots exhibiting hybrid and discontinuous locomotion dynamics, including legged, inchworm-like, and friction-anisotropic climbing platforms. Unlike conventional mobile robots, these systems experience intermittent contact, non-smooth motion transitions, and complex interaction dynamics that challenge existing navigation and mapping algorithms.

The research will investigate probabilistic modeling techniques that explicitly capture hybrid locomotion phases and contact events while integrating information from heterogeneous low-cost sensors, including monocular and stereo cameras, compact LiDARs, inertial measurement units (IMUs), magnetometers, and tactile/contact sensors. Advanced multi-sensor fusion algorithms will be developed to provide accurate and robust state estimation despite limited computational resources and sensing uncertainty.

Furthermore, the study will explore manifold-aware mapping and localization methods that exploit environmental constraints such as walls, pipes, ceilings, and other structured surfaces frequently encountered by climbing and bio-inspired robots. The resulting framework will be optimized for lightweight embedded computing platforms, enabling real-time operation without reliance on high-performance onboard hardware.

The expected outcome is a robust, computationally efficient SLAM and navigation system capable of supporting autonomous exploration, mapping, and localization of miniature robots in complex, cluttered, and visually challenging environments. Applications include infrastructure inspection, search and rescue, industrial monitoring, and environmental exploration in areas inaccessible to conventional robotic systems.

### Key Research Contributions

1. **Hybrid-Dynamics-Aware SLAM**

   * Probabilistic modeling of discontinuous locomotion phases and contact transitions.
   * State estimation that explicitly incorporates gait and contact information.

2. **Resource-Efficient Multi-Sensor Fusion**

   * Fusion of vision, LiDAR, IMU, magnetometer, and tactile sensing.
   * Algorithms designed for embedded and low-power robotic platforms.

3. **Manifold-Constrained Mapping**

   * SLAM on walls, pipes, ceilings, and other constrained surfaces.
   * Geometry-aware map representations for climbing and surface-traversing robots.

4. **Real-Time Embedded Deployment**

   * Optimization for miniature robotic systems with limited computation and energy budgets.
   * Robust operation in low-texture, dynamic, and cluttered environments.

5. **Bio-Inspired Autonomous Navigation**

   * Navigation strategies tailored to legged, inchworm-like, and climbing robots.
   * Improved resilience to slip, contact uncertainty, and intermittent observations.
